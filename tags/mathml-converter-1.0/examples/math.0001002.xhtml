Introduction

This paper examines the relationship between the symplectic quotient X / / G X of a Hamiltonian G G -manifold X X , and the associated symplectic quotient X / / T X , where T ⊂ G ⊂ T G is a maximal torus, in the case in which X / / G X is a compact manifold or orbifold.

The three main results are: a formula expressing the rational cohomology ring of X / / G X in terms of the rational cohomology ring of X / / T X ; an ‘integration’ formula, which expresses cohomology pairings on X / / G X in terms of cohomology pairings on X / / T X ; and an index formula, which expresses the indices of elliptic operators on X / / G X in terms of indices on X / / T X .

The results of this paper are complemented by the results in a companion paper [15], in which different techniques are used to derive formulæ for cohomology pairings on symplectic quotients X / / T X , where T T is a torus, in terms of the T T -fixed points of X X . That paper also gives some applications of the formulæ proved here.

The layout of this paper is as follows. Section  1 contains the main topological result, giving a detailed description of the topological relationship between X / / G X and X / / T X . Section  2 uses this result, together with some cohomological facts, to prove theorem B, the integration formula. In section  3 the integration formula is combined with Poincaré duality to prove theorem A, and in section  4 the index formula is proved. Section  5 is a very short section describing some formulæ for characteristic numbers of X / / G X , such as the Euler characteristic and the signature. In section  6 various generalizations of the main results are described, including straightforward generalizations to the case when the two symplectic quotients are compact orbifolds, to the case in which X / / T X may have singularities or be noncompact, and finally, in a different direction, a generalization in which T T is replaced by a subgroup of full rank. Finally, in section  7 , the results of this paper are applied to the explicit example of the Grassmannian of k k -dimensional planes in C n superscript C n , which arises as a symplectic quotient by the group U ⁢ k U k . The associated symplectic quotient by the maximal torus is the k k -fold product C ⁢ P n - 1 k superscript C superscript P n 1 k . One result is a presentation of the cohomology of the Grassmannian which is different from the usual one.

Relation to other results

The results of this paper, together with the companion paper [15], have been applied by Jeffrey and Kirwan [11] to prove certain formulæ for cohomology pairings on moduli spaces of stable holomorphic bundles over a Riemann surface. These formulæ were first derived by Witten [21] using physical arguments. Indeed the main motivation for the results of this paper and its companion was to find a purely topological proof of Witten’s formulæ.

This work was carried out in 1994, in Oxford, and at the Isaac Newton Institute in Cambridge. After completing this work, I was made aware of some related results of Ellingsrud and Str∘mme [5]. The present paper intersects with theirs in the case that X X is a complex vector space; in that case they have a result closely related to theorem A. (Their general setting is the ‘Geometric invariant theory’ quotient [19] of a vector space over an arbitrary field, for which they calculate the Chow ring; their techniques are completely different from those used here.)
